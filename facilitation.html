Facilitation

Often, clients are referred to me when they are approaching a major Board meeting or a planning retreat and are looking for someone to help provide a framework for the discussion and lead the group to some actionable conclusions.

Clients tell me that they appreciate my flexibility in building an agenda around achieving the meeting's desired outcome. They say they like that I focus on the substance required to get decisions made.

I do this kind of facilitation with organizations that are ready to address the two cornerstone questions of strategy. In some cases, these questions have been addressed and simply need to be clarified or made more specific and actionable. However, skipping these questions altogether is never advisable.
